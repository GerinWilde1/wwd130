Why Visitors Come People will come to my site primarily to explore my coding projects in a structured and accessible way. Potential employers and recruiters will visit to evaluate my technical capabilities and understand how I can contribute to their teams. Colleagues and peers may come to find inspiration or to collaborate on similar projects. Visitor Questions Visitors will likely have several key questions when they arrive at my site. What projects have I worked on? What technologies and programming languages am I proficient in? How can they get in touch with me for potential opportunities or collaborations? Activities Visitors Want Visitors will want to complete several activities on my site, including exploring projects, evaluating skills, reading blog posts, contacting me, and downloading my resume. Project Details To facilitate project exploration, clear, concise descriptions of each project are necessary. These should include objectives, technologies used, key features, and my specific contributions. This detailed information helps visitors understand the scope and depth of my work. ________________________________________ Skills and Experience Evaluating Skills Reviewing my technical skills is a key activity for visitors. They will look at the programming languages, frameworks, tools, and technologies I am proficient in. This section of the site will provide a comprehensive list and possibly examples of how these skills have been applied in my projects. Technical Skills A comprehensive list of programming languages, frameworks, tools, and technologies I'm proficient in is essential. Ease of Communication With easy access to contact information and a streamlined way to reach out, visitors will find it convenient to get in touch for opportunities or questions.
